Humanitarian Response & Crisis Support

Emergency Humanitarian Response – Syrian Earthquake

Jableh, Syria
2023
Volunteer – Community Support & Relief

In the aftermath of the devastating earthquake in February 2023, I joined immediate relief efforts in Jableh, providing direct support to families who lost homes and livelihoods. Working under extreme conditions with limited resources, our team focused on:

  • Distribution of emergency supplies: food, water, blankets, and medical aid
  • Logistics coordination for aid delivery to hardest-hit neighborhoods
  • Emotional and psychological support for traumatized families
  • Damage assessment and prioritization of urgent needs

This experience reinforced my belief that engineering must serve humanity in its moments of greatest need. The resilience I witnessed in my community became a profound source of inspiration for my work in rehabilitation robotics.

Why I Volunteer

"From pain, innovation is born."

Growing up in a conflict-affected country, I witnessed firsthand how adversity can either break or build. I chose the latter. The challenges my community faced became the crucible that shaped my resilience and deepened my commitment to service.

For me, volunteering is not charity—it is responsibility. As an engineer, I believe our greatest innovations must serve humanity's most pressing needs. The earthquake response taught me that technical skills are meaningless unless coupled with empathy and action. The workshops I taught reminded me that knowledge is a gift meant to be shared.

In every competition, every training session, every hour spent organizing academic events, I see an opportunity to build capacity—both in myself and in others. I've learned to smile while solving hard problems, to find hope in difficult circumstances, and to recognize that our shared humanity is our greatest resource.

My journey in robotics and intelligent systems is fundamentally human-centered because I believe technology should amplify our compassion, not replace it. Whether designing rehabilitation devices or teaching programming to youth, the goal remains the same: to create pathways where limitations once existed.

This is not only what I do — this is who I am.
