Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Final Year Thesis | Jan 2023 - Jun 2023

A lightweight, self‑aligning robotic exoskeleton for post‑stroke hand rehabilitation with high‑precision motion tracking.

Full CAD Assembly
3D Printed Components
Mechanical Assembly
Experimental Setup

Technical Details

Mechanical Design

  • Self‑alignment mechanism for anatomical variations
  • Under‑actuated linkage system for natural motion
  • Carbon‑fiber reinforced 3D‑printed structure
  • Total weight: 280g (excluding control unit)

Control System

  • Cascade PID control (position → torque)
  • Real‑time tracking via flex sensors and IMU
  • MATLAB/Simulink for simulation and tuning
  • STM32‑based embedded controller

Results & Impact

Quantitative Results

  • Steady‑state tracking error: <0.5°
  • Settling time: 1.2s (20% faster than baseline)
  • Force control resolution: 0.1N
  • Battery life: 4 hours continuous operation

Innovation

  • Cost reduction: 60% vs commercial systems
  • Novel self‑alignment for user comfort
  • Open‑source control framework

Team Project | Sep 2022 - Dec 2022

System Architecture

  • Perception: RGB‑D camera + thermal sensor for fire detection
  • Navigation: SLAM‑based autonomous navigation
  • Actuation: Water pump with precision nozzle control
  • Control: ROS‑based distributed system

Achievements

  • Fire detection accuracy: 95% at 5m distance
  • Response time: <30s from detection to suppression
  • 1st place in university robotics competition
  • Featured in local news coverage
