Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

Final Year Thesis | 2023

A lightweight, self‑aligning robotic exoskeleton for hand rehabilitation with high‑precision tracking

This project addresses the need for affordable, accessible rehabilitation devices for post‑stroke patients. The system combines mechanical design, control theory, and computer vision to create an intuitive rehabilitation tool.

Key Contributions:

  • Modeling: Developed kinematic and dynamic models of human hand motion
  • Control Strategies: Implemented cascade PID control with gravity compensation
  • Vision‑Based Mirroring: Created camera‑based hand tracking for movement replication
  • Quantitative Results: Achieved <0.5° tracking error with 20% faster settling time
