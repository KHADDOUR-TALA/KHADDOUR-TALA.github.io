Hand Exoskeleton for Rehabilitation

A lightweight, self‑alignment robotic hand exoskeleton designed for safe rehabilitation with high tracking accuracy.

Technical Highlights

  • Modeling & Design: Developed a kinematic model for natural finger motion; designed a self‑alignment mechanism to accommodate anatomical variations.
  • Control: Implemented a PID‑based cascade control strategy for precise torque and position tracking.
  • Sensing: Integrated flex sensors and IMUs for real‑time gesture recognition and feedback.
  • Results: Achieved <0.5° steady‑state tracking error and reduced settling time by 20% compared to a baseline open‑loop controller.

My Role: Lead Designer | System Modeler | Control Algorithm Developer | Experimental Validation.
