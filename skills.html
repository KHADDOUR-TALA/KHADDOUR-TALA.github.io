This Is Me

I was raised in a country marked by conflict, where every day presented new challenges. But I learned early that innovation is often born from pain, and limitations can spark the most creative solutions.

Growing up in Syria, I witnessed how technology could be both a luxury and a lifeline. I saw people with disabilities struggling without access to proper rehabilitation, and I realized that the most meaningful engineering work addresses real human needs.

My journey in robotics began not in a well-equipped lab, but in a small room with limited resources. Every project was a lesson in resourcefulness, every failure a step toward understanding. I learned to see constraints not as barriers, but as design parameters.

I believe that our circumstances don't define us—our response to them does. The challenges I faced taught me resilience, the scarcity taught me creativity, and the human need taught me purpose.

Today, I carry this perspective into every project: technology should serve people, especially those who need it most. My work in rehabilitation robotics is driven by a simple belief—that everyone deserves the dignity of independence, and engineering can help make that possible.

No one defines your destiny except you. Not your background, not your circumstances, not the challenges you face. With hard work, resilience, and self-belief, we can build not just machines, but better futures.

This is my story. This is my mission. This is why I do what I do.

Tala Khaddour

Robotics & Intelligent Systems Engineer
