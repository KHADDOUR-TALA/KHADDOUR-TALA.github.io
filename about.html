About Me

Background

I am a Syrian robotics engineer passionate about creating technological solutions that address real‑world challenges in healthcare and rehabilitation. Growing up in a region with limited access to advanced medical care, I witnessed firsthand the impact that affordable assistive technology could have on individual lives and communities.

My academic journey began with a fascination for mechanics and electronics, which evolved into a dedicated pursuit of mechatronics engineering. At XYZ University, I graduated with Honors, focusing my research on rehabilitation robotics—a field that perfectly combines technical challenge with humanitarian impact.

Research Philosophy

I believe that advanced robotics should not be confined to well‑funded laboratories. My work emphasizes:

  • Accessibility: Designing systems that are cost‑effective and maintainable in resource‑constrained settings
  • User‑Centric Design: Creating interfaces that are intuitive for both patients and clinicians
  • Robust Simplicity: Implementing reliable control strategies that don't require expert operation
  • Local Impact: Developing solutions that address specific regional challenges
